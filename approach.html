Key Educational Theories Behind LexiBridge

1

Communicative Language Teaching (CLT)

Core Idea:

Language is best learned through communication — not just drills. Focus on real-world usage, meaningful interaction, and context.

Relevant Apps:

  • LexiVerse (simulated dialogues, cultural awareness)
  • LexiTales (story-based context learning)
  • LexiWrite (real writing tasks: emails, letters, etc.)

"We focus on language as communication, not just memorization — helping learners use English meaningfully and confidently."

Reference: Canale & Swain

2

Task-Based Language Teaching (TBLT)

Core Idea:

Learners acquire language by doing real-world tasks. Feedback comes from performance, not lectures.

Relevant Apps:

  • LexiWrite (realistic writing scenarios)
  • LexiFlash (time-bound vocab challenges)
  • LexiPlay (educational games and tasks)

"Whether it's writing an email, telling a story, or making a choice in a conversation, every activity is a task with purpose."

Reference: Willis & Willis

3

Input Hypothesis (Stephen Krashen)

Core Idea:

Learners need exposure to language that's just a little above their current level ("i+1"), in a low-stress, comprehensible input environment.

Relevant Apps:

  • LexiTales (narrative input, audio + visual)
  • LexiPlay (gentle vocabulary practice)
  • LexiGrammar (reference, not testing)

"Our apps provide rich, understandable language input—designed to grow with the learner, not overwhelm them."

Reference: Krashen

4

Spaced Repetition + Retrieval Practice

Core Idea:

Memory is strengthened through timed review and active recall.

Relevant Apps:

  • LexiFlash (perfect for spaced recall)
  • LexiVerse (review differences across time/regions)
  • LexiTales (Comprehension questions, pronunciation practice )

"We use smart repetition and quick quizzes to help knowledge stick—long after the app is closed."

Reference: Roediger & Karpicke

5

Multimodal Learning

Core Idea:

Learners retain more when information comes through multiple senses—reading, listening, speaking, watching.

Relevant Apps:

  • LexiTales (text + audio + visuals)
  • LexiPlay (sound, interaction)
  • LexiGrammar (visual reference)

"Every Lexi app combines sound, visuals, and interaction—so learners can understand and remember better."

Reference: Mayer's Cognitive Theory of Multimedia Learning

"The best language learning doesn't feel like studying—it feels like living, playing, and communicating naturally."

— Dr. Amelia Chen, LexiBridge Educational Director
